My Approach

Empowerment through Research: I dive deep into the intricacies of urban environments, harnessing advanced analytical tools like STATA, R, SQL, and Atlas.ti to craft policies that foster equitable living conditions for all.

Sustainable and Cost-Effective Living:

  • Energy Transition: I advocate for the integration of renewable energy solutions, such as solar panels and geothermal systems, not only to reduce energy costs but also to elevate air quality for residents.
  • Environmental Considerations: My designs incorporate green roofs, rain gardens, and other sustainable features, reducing ecological footprints while protecting communities from climate extremes.
  • Cost Reduction: By focusing on low-impact materials and efficient construction methods, my approach ensures homes are not only environmentally friendly but also economically viable.

Resilient and Inclusive Communities: I push for policies that guarantee safe, sustainable, and affordable housing, ensuring everyone has a stake in their community's future.

My vision extends to creating neighborhoods that are more than just homes; they are vibrant ecosystems with community centers, green spaces, and local markets, enhancing social cohesion and opportunity.

Advocacy and Action: My work goes beyond research; I actively engage with urban planning to ensure developments are inclusive, reflecting the needs of the underrepresented. I partner with community groups, NGOs, and policymakers to challenge and change systemic housing inequalities.

Innovation in Housing Solutions: I pioneer innovative housing models like co-housing and modular constructions, providing living spaces that are not only cost-effective but also adaptable to the community's evolving needs.

Educational Outreach: I empower communities through education, ensuring they have the knowledge to maintain sustainable practices, making sustainability both accessible and practical.

The Five A's of Housing:

  • Accessible: Ensuring housing is reachable for all, regardless of socio-economic status.
  • Acceptable: Guaranteeing homes meet high standards of livability and dignity.
  • Adaptable: Designing spaces that can evolve with the changing needs of residents.
  • Affordable: Making sure cost does not prohibit access to decent housing.
  • Available: Increasing the supply of housing where it's needed most.
